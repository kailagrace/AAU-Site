Counterfeit, recycled and manipulated computer chips are a major concern in the international electronic component supply chain with the potential to disrupt national transportation, financial, energy, and military systems.

Researchers from UConn’s Center for Hardware Assurance, Security, and Engineering (CHASE) have secured millions of dollars in funding to develop innovative technologies and detection techniques in order to ensure that today’s integrated circuits and computer systems are reliable and secure. This is just one way that UConn is unleashing the solutions of tomorrow.

Cyber-security presents challenges. UConn develops solutions. We code it. We get it.

We create it.

Robert Howe, a doctoral candidate in music history and music theory at UConn, is collaborating with Sina Shahbazmohamadi, UConn’s director of advanced 3D imaging, and music history professor Richard Bass to precisely measure parts of antique instruments such as the saxophone and recorder using medical scanning technology. They are replicating instrument parts for the first time using 3-D printing, which could allow instruments hundreds of years old to be played once again while providing security authentication for rare instruments held by collectors and museums.

This is just one way that UConn is unleashing the solutions of tomorrow.

We prevent it.

The Korey Stringer Institute, part of UConn’s College of Agriculture, Health, and Natural Resources, provides up to date information, resources, assistance and advocacy for the prevention of sudden death in sport. Chief operating officer and kinesiology professor Douglas J. Casa is an advocate for prevention measures that can protect athletes, soldiers, and laborers from heat stroke and other exercise-related illnesses that can cause sudden death or long-term disability. A fellow of the National Athletic Trainers’ Association, and the American College of Sports Medicine, Casa has been featured in Sports Illustrated, the New York Times, ESPN, NPR, CNN, USA Today, NBC’s Today Show and PBS Frontline among many other media publications nationally and internationally. His passion is inspiring, and unleashing the solutions of tomorrow.

Running interference on athlete’s safety enables UConn to protect and save lives. We prevent it. We get it.

As a featured commentator on MSNBC, National Public Radio, CNN, Al-Jazeera, CBS News, the NY Times, and numerous other national outlets, Cobb has the attention of an international audience. His opinions are shaping society and unleashing the solutions of tomorrow.
